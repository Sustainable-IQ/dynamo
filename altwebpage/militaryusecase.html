The Dynamic AI Memory Organizer Platform

The Dynamic AI Memory Organizer (DYNAMO) is a platform that transforms your conversations — and your community's — with large language models (LLMs) into persistent, living memory. It doesn't just store dialogue, it uses those memories to guide growth, spark insight, and weave collective wisdom. Built on a Self-Organizing Holarchic Open (SOHO) architecture, DYNAMO turns dialogue into continuity, and continuity into transformation — supporting personal development, long-term coherence, and community intelligence.

Understanding DYNAMO's Defense Application

What This Is About

This document presents DYNAMO's application to military and intelligence operations. Traditional AI chatbots can't be used in classified environments because they lack proper security controls, don't maintain memory across sessions, and can't enforce who sees what information. DYNAMO solves this by bringing conversational AI into secure military networks (called IL5/IL6 classified enclaves) with enterprise-grade security and memory that persists over time.

The Core Problem

Military personnel need AI assistance for decision-making, but commercial AI services like ChatGPT cannot be used with classified information. Current solutions either lack proper access controls, don't maintain continuity of knowledge, or can't meet Department of Defense security requirements. This creates a gap between the AI capabilities available to the commercial world and what's available to warfighters and intelligence analysts.

DYNAMO's Military Solution

What makes DYNAMO different:

  • Deployed in secure enclaves: Runs entirely within classified networks (IL5/IL6) with no connection to the public internet, keeping sensitive information completely isolated.
  • Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC): Automatically controls who can see what based on rank, security clearance, unit assignment, and mission need-to-know. A Private sees different information than a Colonel.
  • Audit-first memory: Every conversation, every decision, and every piece of information is logged with complete traceability. You can reconstruct who asked what, when, and what answer they received.
  • Council-based AI agents: Instead of one generic AI, DYNAMO provides specialized AI agents aligned to military structure (enlisted, officer, warrant officer, etc.), each with appropriate knowledge and guardrails.
  • Living memory with provenance: Information isn't just stored—it's tracked from source to usage. If a document is revoked or updated, the system knows and can prevent outdated information from being used.
  • DoD compliance ready: Built to meet Risk Management Framework (RMF) requirements, with Security Technical Implementation Guide (STIG) compliance, and a path to Authority to Operate (ATO) certification.

In plain terms: DYNAMO brings ChatGPT-like capabilities to classified military operations, but with the security, access controls, audit trails, and compliance that the Department of Defense requires. It remembers context over time, enforces who can access what information, and maintains complete accountability for every interaction.

Capacity Planning (honest math)

Measure tokens/sec per inference pod in pilot using your real prompts; size pods to hit p95 ≤ 10s at target concurrency.

Pods ≈ ceil((C × K × A) / (T × U × SLO_seconds))
Where: C=concurrency, K=tokens/turn, A=agents, T=tok/s/pod, U=utilization

Plain‑Language FAQ

Is this “ChatGPT for SIPR”?
No. Generic SaaS chat is restricted. DYNAMO is enclave‑resident with ABAC/RBAC, provenance & revocation, and an RMF/eMASS path.
How do we prevent spillage?
Derivative classification safeguards, portion marks, REL TO/NOFORN enforcement, automated quarantine, and auditable traceability.
How is access controlled?
Attribute‑based at query time using rank, billet, unit, clearance, compartments, caveats, and mission need‑to‑know; deny‑by‑default.
Where does this go after pilot?
Scale by enclave and mission packs; optional coalition “REL TO” enclaves with per‑tenant keys and data segregation.
